About Admission

University admission prediction utilizes a variety of data points including academic performance, standardized test scores, extracurricular activities, and personal statements to forecast a student's likelihood of acceptance. Machine learning algorithms are often employed in university admission prediction models to analyze historical admission data and identify patterns that can help predict future outcomes for applicants. While admission prediction can offer insights into a student's chances of acceptance, it's important to remember that each university's selection process is multifaceted and subjective, with factors beyond quantifiable metrics influencing final decisions.

Algorithm

Regression algorithms aim to model the relationship between dependent and independent variables, enabling prediction of continuous outcomes. Popular techniques include linear regression, which assumes a linear relationship, and more complex models like polynomial regression or support vector regression.

Accuracy

In regression algorithms, accuracy is typically measured by metrics such as Mean Squared Error (MSE), Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE), or R-squared. These metrics quantify the difference between predicted and actual values, providing a measure of how well the model fits the data. Higher accuracy, indicated by lower error values or higher R-squared values, suggests that the regression model effectively captures the relationship between variables and makes more precise predictions.
